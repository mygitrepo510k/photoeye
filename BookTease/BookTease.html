Robert A. Sobieszek: Ghost in the Shell
Published in conjunction with a Los Angeles County Museum of Art exhibition of the same name, Ghost in the Shell takes a deeper look at portraiture, collecting works in search of the inner experience. In three heavily, inventively illustrated essays, the authors relate 150 years of photographers' searches for the soul. From Nadar and the medical photographs of Duchenne de Boulogne to Cindy Sherman and Andy Warhol, no stone remains unturned.

Josef Koudelka: Chaos
The most comprehensive collection of Koudelka's panoramas ever published, this finely produced, oversized monograph is an essential part of the photographer's bibliography. After twenty years as a photojournalist helping to define the Magnum style - documenting gypsies of Eastern Europe, the Soviet invasion of Prague, and European exiles - Koudelka turned to the panoramic camera and with it the landscape, demonstrating the same intelligence and conviction that informed his earlier work.
