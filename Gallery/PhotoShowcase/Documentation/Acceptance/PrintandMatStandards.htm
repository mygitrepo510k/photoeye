Represented Artists

All prints sold must be processed to professional archival standards. Prints must have an unblemished surface with careful spotting of all imperfections. All prints must be signed and dated, and professionally matted using museum or 100% rag board. Optionally, photo-eye may mat your prints with the fee deducted from the sale price. See matting fees below.

Print Quality Standards

  • Archival Processing
    All work must be processed to archival standards.

  • Professional Processing
    All work must be in saleable condition, with an unblemished print surface and careful spotting of dust and imperfections.

  • Sign your work
    Sign and date your prints in pencil either on the front or the back. Do not sign the mat!

  • Editioned Prints
    If you limit your editions, note your edition numbers in pencils. Example: 1/5. Do not put edition numbers on the mat!

  • Mat Standards
     - Professional mats using conservation or 100% rag board
    - No over cuts in corners of the mat cutouts
    - Straight cuts with clean bevels
    - Sufficient margins to float the print
    - No print corners should be viewable
    - Professional dry mounting of work is acceptable if
       archival materials are used
    - Do not sign the mat
    - Do not create a window for your signature
