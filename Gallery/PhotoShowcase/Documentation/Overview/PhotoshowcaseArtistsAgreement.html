  • Overview
    Photographer’s Showcase is a juried online exhibition and sales site for serious, fine art photographers from around the world. It is integral to our high traffic website photoeye.com. The application process includes a one-time, nonrefundable jury fee. If accepted and one agrees to exhibit, there will be an exhibition fee based on the number of jury selected images shown. The artist may re-apply at any time with new images.

  • Terms of Agreement
    The initial agreement between the artist and photo-eye begins immediately from the date that both parties sign the contract. The contract ends upon completion of the one-year exhibition period, which begins on the date the artist’s showcase is publicly launched.

  • Exhibition Fees
    Click Here for Exhibition Fees

  • Non-Exclusive Agreement
    photo-eye wants the artist to be successful in their career. Therefore, photo-eye does not require an exclusive contract with Photographer’s Showcase. The artist is free to choose other non-exclusive exhibition arenas, giving their work even more visibility.

  • Renewal of Agreement
    45 days before the contract ends, both parties will have an opportunity to renew. If both parties agree, then a new one-year contract is signed.

  • Ownership of Images and Copyright
    The artist is the sole and exclusive owner of the copyright of all images they create, and that are part of this agreement. photo-eye has the right to offer these copyrighted works for sale on Photographer’s Showcase.

  • Copyright Protection
    The artist should understand that the presentation of his/her images on the Internet enables the public to download their images. The artist, not photo-eye, may legally challenge any copyright infringements, and will assume responsibility for any financial costs in such pursuits. The artist would be the sole recipient of all proceeds gained from such infringements.

  • Communication with Artist
    photo-eye will communicate with artists by e-mail, phone, or fax. Artists are asked to have a working e-mail address, voice mail, or fax number that is checked regularly.

  • Promotion
    photoeye.com is promoted in a variety of ways, which may include advertising in national and local media, issuing press releases, and visibility in the photo-eye mailorder catalog which is sent to over 12,000 individuals worldwide - the heart of the fine art photographic community. photo-eye often uses images on photoeye.com in these promotions. When photo-eye uses, copies, distributes, publishes, and publicly displays any Showcase images for the purpose of marketing and selling the artworks, then the artists will be contacted for approval.

  • Referral credits
    Artists who exhibit their work on the Photographers’s Showcase may receive up to $15 for each new artist referred to Showcase. Credits apply to any future Exhibition Fees. Click for Referral Credits information.

  • Print Prices
    The artist determines the retail price for their artwork, subject to approval, however it should remain consistent in all venues. When determining the price, the Represented Artist should keep in mind that all matting, insurance, and shipping fees to Santa Fe, NM, USA are absorbed by the artist. The minimum price for each artwork shown on the Photographer’s Showcase is US $200.

  • Discounts on Sales of Represented Artists Works
    Occasionally discounts on prints are given to museums, other galleries, interior designers, dealers, or authorized agents. Discounts range between 5 and 15%. Since photo-eye only takes a 30% commission on sales, any discount 10% or below will be deducted from the artist's percentage. Any discount above 10% will be split between the artist and photo-eye. For example, if a 20% discount is given to a museum, then photo-eye will absorb 10% and the artist 10%, resulting in a 20% commission paid to photo-eye instead of the usual 30%. If a 10% discount is given to a collector, then the artist will absorb the entire discount and photo-eye will receive the normal 30% commission. No prior approval from the artist is needed in order to discount prints.

  • Print and Matting Specifications for Represented Artists
    All prints sold must be processed to professional archival standards. Prints must have an unblemished surface with careful spotting of all imperfections. All prints must be signed and dated, and professionally matted using museum or 100% rag board. Click Here for Matting or Shipping Standards.

  • Shipping Artwork - Represented Artists
    All prints must be packed to minimize the risk of damage while in transit. We highly recommend that you use a durable outside carton such as, Print Pad available from Air Float Systems, Inc. (800-445-2580 or www.airfloatsys.com). In addition to the outside carton, your artwork should be placed in a plastic archival print bag to help prevent abrasion of the print surface. One source for such bags is Light Impressions, Inc. (800-828-6216 or www.lightimpressionsdirect.com).

  • Insurance - Represented Artist
    The artist is responsible for insuring their artwork from their address to photo-eye. Once the artwork is in the possession of photo-eye, they are fully insured for liability and shipping to the buyer.

  • Returns (Represented Artists)
    photoeye.com will accept all returns under our satisfaction guarantee policy. All customers who notify us within 14 days from the date of shipment and return the artwork in the same condition, will receive a full refund less shipping. The artwork is then returned to the artist at photo-eye’s expense and no payment to the artist is made. No reimbursement is made for any shipping expense from the artist to photo-eye.
