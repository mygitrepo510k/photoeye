Print Sales
  • Will my work be sold online? If so, how much of a commission does photo-eye take?
    Represented Artists

    Photographer’s Showcase is an e-commerce site with opportunities for print sales. photo-eye acts as your agent in fielding queries, securing sales, and doing follow-up on orders. Prints are not consigned, instead they are ordered on a special order basis. photo-eye is responsible for collecting payment and servicing clients. Your print price, size, title, and medium is clearly visible with each print shown online. A 30% commission is taken on all sales.
    Independent Photographers

    Sales contacts by the buyer are made directly with the photographer. Your print price, size, title, and medium is clearly visible with each print shown online. The customer clicks the Contact Photographer link to email their requests. If a sale is made, no commission is taken by photo-eye.

  • How do customers place an order for prints on the website?

    Represented Artists
    A "Place an Order" link is prominently displayed in the Artist's portfolio next to the image being shown. By clicking this link, customers follow a simple, secure step-by-step procedure to place an order.

    Independent Photographers
    A "Contact Photographer" link is prominently displayed in the Photographer's portfolio next to the image being shown. An email form pops up allowing the customer to contact the photographer directly.

  • Can customers place orders to my book through my portfolio or must they leave the galleries and enter the bookstore?

    photo-eye has the largest selection of in-print fine-art photography books in the world. There are links from your online portfolio to any books containing your work that are listed in our database, and vice versa. Customers are able to purchase books when they click on the link to your book in our bookstore without leaving your portfolio.

  • When do I get paid for prints that have sold?

    Represented Artists
    The artist is paid 45 days after the artwork is shipped to the buyer.

    Independent Photographers
    Since sales are made directly from the customer to you, arrangements for payment for any print sales are made by you.

  • Does photoeye.com accept credit cards online?

    Represented Artists
    Yes and it is safe. Our secure e-commerce server is encrypted so that customer information including credit card numbers cannot be copied outside of photoeye.com.

    Independent Photographers
    You are free to make sales any way you can.

  • How will I know when a print has sold?

    Represented Artists
    When a sale is made either online or by phone, photo-eye sends the artist a purchase order by e-mail, fax or phone. The artist ships the matted artwork to photo-eye in Santa Fe, NM. photo-eye approves the print quality and presentation, then sends the artwork to the buyer. As part of the online ordering process, customers should expect to receive their print(s) within 2-4 weeks of placing an order. However, if a photographer is unable to deliver a print to photo-eye within a reasonable period, then photo-eye will inform the customer of such delays.

    Independent Photographers
    Sales are made directly. You will receive an email from the client who is interested in your work.

  • What if I am out of town or on an extended trip when a print sells?
    If you are going to be out of town for an extended period, we encourage you to communicate your leave of absence with someone at the telephone and/or email that you have provided. There is no need to e-mail or contact us before you leave or when you return unless you need to change your contact email, address, or phone.

  • May I ever speak directly with a client?

    Represented Artists
    No. photo-eye will act as your agent. If a customer has any questions, we will contact you.

    Independent Photographers
    Yes. You are dealing directly with the client.

  • What if a client wishes to see work that is not included in the Showcase?

    Represented Artists
    photo-eye acts as your agent. If a customer requests to see other work, we will contact you to send images.
    Independent Photographers
    You may link your portfolio to your website and send additional additional images to them any way you choose.

  • Do you ever offer discounts to clients, and if so, how do you handle discounts?
    Represented Artists
    Occasionally discounts on prints are given to museums, other galleries, interior designers, dealers, or authorized agents. Discounts range between 5 and 15%. Since photo-eye only takes a 30% commission on sales, any discount 10% or below will be deducted from the artist's percentage. Any discount above 10% will be split between the artist and photo-eye. For example, if a 20% discount is given to a museum, then photo-eye will absorb 10% and the artist 10%, resulting in a 20% commission paid to photo-eye instead of the usual 30%. If a 10% discount is given to a collector, then the artist will absorb the entire discount and photo-eye will receive the normal 30% commission. No prior approval from the artist is needed in order to discount prints.

  • Can I get credit in the photo-eye bookstore or the other galleries instead of a check for my sold prints?
    For accounting purposes, the Photographer’s Showcase and our other online galleries at photoeye.com, and the bookstore are separate divisions of photo-eye Books & Prints. You are welcome to place orders at anytime in either the bookstore or other galleries, but we cannot issue book or print credit for prints sold.

  • What if I edition my prints – how will I advise you of price increases?
    You do not have to contact us, rather use your personal access password that allows you to make any changes to your prints by yourself.

  • Do I have to sell my work in numbered editions?
    No. Editioning of work is a personal, artistic choice.

  • Why would a photographer edition work?
    There are both advantages and disadvantages to editioning one’s work. Typically photographers will edition their work in numbers ranging from 1-50 prints. Collectors often feel reassured when an image is editioned since they know that only a set number of prints will ever be on the market. In reality though, only the most popular prints ever sell out of their edition. Editions with a prices that change based on the number of prints sold encourages collectors to buy new work instead of their more expensive, popular imagery.

    The disadvantage of editioning an image is that keeping track of numbers in the edition can be cumbersome and some perceive that it artificially inflates the market.

  • How do I edition my work?
    With a pencil, print the number of the print / edition ceiling (e.g. 1/5) on the front or back of the actual photograph. Do not put edition numbers on the mat.

  • Does my work have to sell for the same amount in all galleries?
    Yes. To be professional, you should sell your prints for the same price everywhere.

  • Does photo-eye have to approve my prices?
    No, unless the staff feels it is unreasonable. The artist determines the retail price for their artwork, however it should remain consistent in all venues. When determining the price, you should keep in mind that all matting, insurance, and shipping feels to Santa Fe, NM, USA are absorbed by the artist. For Represented Artists, remember that photo-eye takes a 30% commission on sales. Your work should be priced according to what the market will bear, neither too low or too high. However the minimum price allowed on the site is $200.

  • Do I pay shipping costs to you and then again to the client?
    No. You pay for the shipping and insurance costs only to photo-eye in Santa Fe, NM.

  • Can clients return Represented Artists prints?
    Yes. photo-eye will accept all prints under our satisfaction guarantee policy. The client has 14 days from the date of shipping to return the artwork to photo-eye in the same condition. The artwork is then returned to the artist at photo-eye’s expense.

  • Can I show my work without making it available for sale? What if an image I want to show is already sold out?
    Yes. You may show work that is not for sale. Choose the proper Not-for-Sale option on your online administration form for that particular print.

  • If an art director wants to hire me to create new work based on seeing my page on the Photographer’s Showcase, am I obligated to give photo-eye a percentage of creative fees realized?
    No. We act as your agent and will take a commission only if your are a Represented Artist and the sale is made through photo-eye.

  • If someone sees my work and contacts me directly am I obligated to give photo-eye a percentage of the sale?
    No. None whatsoever.
