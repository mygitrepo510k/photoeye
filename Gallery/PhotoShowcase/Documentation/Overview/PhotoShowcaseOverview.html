O V E R V I E W

The first and foremost reason to become a Showcase photographer is that your work will be part of a stunning, highly respected, fine-art photography site that has a tremendous world-wide audience.

Over the past twenty-one years, photo-eye has grown to become the premier source for fine-art photographic books. Over the past ten years, photo-eye Gallery has shown exciting contemporary photography in our formal gallery in Santa Fe. Our mailorder catalogues are sent quarterly to a worldwide audience and include the largest selection of art photography books found anywhere. photoeye.com is the one of the web's foremost e-commerce sites devoted to fine-art photography.

photo-eye Gallery, Santa Fe, like most galleries, can represent relatively few artists. With the advent of the Internet, photo-eye is now able to show diverse work by serious, fine-art photographers from throughout the world. Our new online gallery, the Photographer’s Showcase, lets photographers participate by uploading, describing, and maintaining their own portfolios online, then showing that work to photo-eye's international audience. Our demographics include curators, gallery directors, collectors, teachers, and other lovers of fine-art photography, over two thousand visitors a day, nearly a million visitors a year, the perfect audience to view portfolios of your work!

photo-eye invites artists to submit work to be juried for this exciting new exhibition space. The Showcase is looking for quality artwork that utilizes photographic methods, either exclusively or in conjunction with other artistic processes. The successful portfolio is a cohesive body of work that demonstrates a freshness of vision. photo-eye is interested in showing personal artistic work, not commercial or stock photography. Acceptance is based on the quality of the work, not professional stature.

Here's how it works...
  • Apply Online
    It's simple, fast, and with application forms and an image upload system all online.
    It costs as little as $185 for one year to be a Showcase photographer.
    - Pay a $35 application fee for the first portfolio, $20 for each additional portfolio (if submitted at the same time).
    - Fill out the bio, artist and process statements, and title list online.
    - Upload up to 20 scanned images, for each portfolio submitted, in the form of ~200k JPEGS.
    - Notify us by email when the application is complete.

  • Work is Juried
    photo-eye juries the portfolios and chooses photographers to show online. There are two classifications for photographers accepted into the Showcase: Represented and Independent.

    Represented
    The jury decides if a photographer is accepted into the Showcase Galleries as a Represented Artist. If so, they are included in either Showcase Gallery One - Established Artists or Showcase Gallery Two - Emerging Artists. Our experienced photo-eye Gallery staff in Santa Fe acts as the artist's agent, and is responsible for pre-sales customer service and post-sales follow-up. The Gallery commission is 30%. The work is offered for sale online through our secure server ordering system.

    Independent
    The jury decides if a photographer is accepted into Showcase Gallery Three - Independent Photographers. If accepted, some or all of the portfolio images submitted to the jury may be shown. The photographer acts as an independent artist instead of being represented by photo-eye. Independent artists have the freedom to develop their own customer relations and sell prints directly to clients. No commission is taken by photo-eye. Direct customer contact means that clients reach artists through email, rather than through photo-eye's online print purchasing system. Independent artists are encouraged to link their portfolios to their own personal web sites.

  • Juror's Criteria
    Showcase Gallery One - Established Artists
    Includes photo-eye Online Representation
    Artists are included whose work has an obvious long-term acceptance by the fine-art photographic community. This may include some or all of the following: major museum shows, significant monographs, and representation by at least two AIPAD (Association of International Photography Art Dealers) Galleries.

    Showcase Gallery Two - Emerging Artists
    Includes photo-eye Online Representation
    Artists included are those that the jury feels exhibit an exceptional freshness of vision, along with a seriousness of intent, which is consistent throughout their submitted portfolio. This includes documentary projects that may or may not be published elsewhere. These artists do not yet qualify as Established Artists.

    Showcase Gallery Three - Independent Photographers
    Artists who show an obvious commitment to photography through the love of craft and medium, with some strong individual images. There is no photo-eye Online representation. Instead the photographers may sell directly.

  • Portfolios are Setup
    Once accepted by the jury, the photographer signs the artist agreement and pays the setup and exhibition fees. photo-eye then assigns the initial password so that the artist may access their portfolio setup pages. The artist fills out their résumé, chronology, and artist statements online, sequences their images, and includes descriptive information about each print, all online.

  • Portfolios are Exhibited
    photo-eye reviews the online portfolios before giving final approval. The work is then made live and immediately integrates with the rest of the photo-eye Galleries at photoeye.com. The one year non-exclusive contract begins.

  • When Sales are Made
    Represented Artists
    photo-eye Gallery Santa Fe, acts as the artist's agent, taking a 30% commission on any sales. When a sale is made either online or by phone, photo-eye orders the print. The artist ships the matted artwork to photo-eye in Santa Fe, NM, USA. All matting, insurance, and shipping fees to Santa Fe are absorbed by the artist. photo-eye approves the print quality and presentation, then sends the artwork to the buyer. The buyer has a 14 day satisfaction guarantee return privilege.

    Independent Artists
    The artist sells directly to the client. If a client is interested in purchasing a print, they click the Contact Artist link next to the print shown in the Independent Artist's portfolio. The artist is responsible for all pre-sales and post-sales customer service. A photo-eye disclaimer is shown when the Contact Artist link is clicked, releasing photo-eye from any involvement or liability in any sales that result.

  • When Rights are Sold
    Represented Artists
    photo-eye acts as the artist's agent negotiating any sales of rights on the artist's behalf with the artist's approval.

    Independent Artists
    The artist negotiates any sales of rights to their own images.
