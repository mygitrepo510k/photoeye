Frequently Asked Questions

I. Overview

What is the Photographer’s Showcase?

A juried online print exhibition and sales space at photoeye.com that showcases serious, fine-art photographers from throughout the world.

Who should apply?

Emerging and mid-career artists who want to exhibit and sell their work on a highly visible fine-art photography website. The Showcase is looking for personal, artistic work that demonstrates a freshness of vision. Acceptance is based on the quality of work, not professional stature.

Why should artists consider an online venue for exhibiting and selling their work?

The barriers for entry into a formal photography or art gallery are extremely high. Each time a gallery represents a new artist, a major commitment of time and money is made. The photo-eye Gallery in Santa Fe can only handle the work of twenty or so artists due to space limitations. Now with the advent of the Internet, we are able to show work by many talented photographers, allowing their portfolios to be viewed by thousands of visitors each day. The Gallery at photo-eye receives requests to look at new work each day, the Photographer’s Showcase now provides a place for high quality work to be shown.

How will the Photographer’s Showcase be advertised?

The Photographer’s Showcase will be promoted in a variety of ways, including advertising in national and local media, and issuing press releases. Marketing will also be in the photo-eye booklist, a mailorder catalogue that is sent bi-monthly to a worldwide audience and includes the largest selection of fine-art photography books available anywhere. Whenever images from the Showcase are promoted in the booklist, credit is given to the artist.

 

II. Eligibility and Acceptance Criteria

Who is eligible for the Photographer’s Showcase?

The Showcase is looking for quality artwork that utilizes photographic methods, either exclusively or in conjunction with other artistic processes. The successful portfolio is a cohesive body of personal, artistic work that demonstrates a freshness of vision. Commercial or stock photography should not be submitted.

What if I’ve only recently begun working with photography? Is there a required amount of time that I need to have worked with photography before applying?

We are interested in photographers who are serious about their work and who are creating fresh, original images. Whether you are new to the art of photography or a veteran, we look forward to seeing your work, and encourage you to apply.

Do I have to have gallery representation elsewhere before I may apply?

No gallery representation or prior experience with galleries is required. If you are new to exhibiting your work, we encourage you to apply.

Does my work have to be published in a book for me to apply?

Not at all. However if a book has been published, please submit it along with your application. It will help us better understand the scope of your work.

I’m going to be in Santa Fe soon. May I personally present work for review?

Yes. Original prints, slides, and scans may be accepted in person, though we cannot guarantee that it be viewed the same day.

If accepted, will my work be in any shows in the Santa Fe Gallery?

Through the Showcase, other galleries, museums, magazines and publishers view your work. Our formal photo-eye Gallery in Santa Fe will exhibit one Showcase artist each year. There is always the chance that other venues will want to handle your work.

If accepted, am I represented by the photo-eye Gallery?

The Photographer’s Showcase at photo-eye.com has a highly visible presence as one of three distinct exhibition spaces. The other spaces include photo-eye Represented Artists from the Santa Fe Gallery and Magnum Photos Collectors Prints. If accepted you will be an exhibiting photographer on the Photographer’s Showcase at photoeye.com, not photo-eye Gallery.

Is there a chance that I will become a represented artist at photo-eye Gallery?

Currently, photo-eye Gallery is not reviewing portfolios and is not taking on new artists.

Is the Showcase a non-exclusive agreement, meaning I can show work at other galleries?

Yes, photo-eye wants you to be successful as an artist. Unlike many online venues, we do not require that you sign an exclusive contract. Thus you are free to choose other non-exclusive exhibition areas, giving your work even more visibility. Our contract allows artists to show their work in galleries around the country and on other Internet sites.

V. Print Sales

Will my work be sold online?

Yes. Photographer’s Showcase is an e-commerce site with opportunities for print sales. photo-eye acts as your agent in fielding queries, securing sales, and follow-up on orders. Prints are not consigned, instead they are ordered on a special order basis. photo-eye is responsible for collecting payment and servicing clients. Your print price, size, title, and medium is clearly visible with each print shown online.

Does photo-eye take a commission on print sales, like other galleries, and if so, how

much?

Yes. photo-eye takes a 40% commission on all print sales.

How do customers place an order on the website?

A "Place an Order" link is prominently displayed on each image page. By clicking this link, customers follow a simple step-by-step procedure to place an order. photo-eye has the largest selection of in-print fine-art photography books in the world. There are links from your online portfolio to any books containing your work that are listed in our database, and vice versa. Customers are able to purchase prints and books on their same order.

When do I get paid for prints that have sold?

The artist is paid 45 days after the artwork is shipped to the buyer.

Does photo-eye.com accept credit cards online?

Yes and it is safe. Our secure e-commerce server is encrypted so that customer information including credit card numbers cannot be copied outside of photo-eye.com.

How will I know when a print has sold?

photo-eye informs customers that, in general, they can expect to receive their print within 2-3 weeks of placing an order. When a sale is made either online or by phone, photo-eye sends the artist a purchase order by e-mail, fax or phone. The artist ships the matted artwork to photo-eye in Santa Fe, NM. photo-eye approves the print quality and presentation, then sends the artwork to the buyer.

What if I am out of town or on an extended trip when a print sells?

If you are going to be out of town for an extended period, we encourage you to communicate when you expect to return with someone at the telephone and/or email that you have provided. There is no need to e-mail or contact us before you leave or when you return.

May I ever speak directly with a client?

No. photo-eye will act as your agent. If a customer has any questions, we will contact you.

What if a client wishes to see work that is not included in the Showcase?

photo-eye acts as your agent. If a customer requests to see other work, we will contact you and ask you to send images.

Do you ever offer discounts to clients, and if so, how do you handle discounts?

Occasionally discounts on prints are given to museums, other galleries, interior designers, dealers, or authorized agents. Discounts range between 5 and 20%, split evenly between the photographer and photo-eye. For example, if a 10% discount is given to a museum, then photo-eye will absorb 5% and the artist 5%, resulting in a 35% commission instead of the usual 40%. No prior approval from the artist is needed in order to discount prints.

What if I edition my prints – how will I advise you of price increases?

You do not have to contact us, rather use your personal access password that allows you to make any changes to your prints by yourself.

Do I have to sell my work in numbered editions?

No. Editioning of work is a personal, artistic choice.

Why would a photographer edition work?

There are both advantages and disadvantages to editioning one’s work. Typically photographers will edition their work in numbers ranging from 10-50 prints. Collectors often feel reassured when an image is editioned because they know that only a set number of prints will ever be on the market. In reality though, it is unusual for prints to sell out in their edition. The disadvantage of editioning an image is that keeping track of numbers in the edition can be cumbersome.

How do I edition my work?

With a pencil, print the number of the print / edition ceiling (e.g. 1/5).

Does my work have to sell for the same amount in all galleries?

Yes. To be professional, you should sell your prints for the same price everywhere.

Does photo-eye have to approve my prices?

No. The artist determines the retail price for their artwork, however it should remain consistent in all venues. When determining the price, you should keep in mind that all matting, insurance, and shipping feels to Santa Fe, NM, USA are absorbed by the artist. Also, we take a 40% commission on sales. Your work should be priced according to what the market will bear, neither too low or too high.

Do I pay shipping costs to you and then again to the client?

No. You pay for the shipping and insurance costs only to photo-eye in Santa Fe, NM.

Can clients return prints?

Yes. photo-eye will accept all prints under our satisfaction guarantee policy. The client has 14 days from the date of shipping to return the artwork to photo-eye in the same condition. The artwork is then returned to the artist at photo-eye’s expense.

Print and Mat Standards

Do sold prints have to be matted?

Yes. As a protection for the print and the aesthetics of the work, we require that prints sold must be matted. The price of the print should include the cost of the mat. Please price your work accordingly.

What are your matting requirements?

We accept only 2 or 4 ply museum rag or conservation board for our mats. This board is readily available at many art supply stores as well as Light Impressions, a mail order supplier of archival materials. Windows should be cleanly cut with a 45 degree bevel. Windows must be hinged to a backing board using archival tape. Photographs can be either dry mounted or held in place with corners.

What if my work is framed?

We accept only matted prints. Frames are cumbersome and place the print in risk of damage during shipping.

What if I don’t know how to mat my own work?

Visit a frame shop in your hometown for advice or to employ them to cut mats for you. Insist on museum quality board.

Shipping Standards

How do I best protect my work when I send it to you?

All prints must be packed to minimize the risk of damage while in transit. We highly recommend that you use a durable outside carton such as, Print Pad available from Air Float Systems, Inc. (800-445-2580 or www.airfloatsys.com). In addition to the outside carton, your artwork should be placed in a plastic archival print bag to help prevent abrasion of the print surface. One source for such bags is Light Impressions, Inc. (800-828-6216 or www.lightimpressionsdirect.com).

 

Technical Issues

I am not very adept at the computer and am worried that my scans will not accurately translate the quality of my work? What should I do?

The Showcase is a database driven site that must meet our strict requirements for size and resolution. The highest quality scans are made from original prints, second best are scans made from excellent slide reproductions, and the third choice for quality scans is from printed reproductions. photo-eye reserves the right to refuse any scan that does not meet the Showcase standard. We will approve all scans prior to showing the work online. If you are interested in having photo-eye build your site, please review our Help Building Showcase form for technical service fees.
