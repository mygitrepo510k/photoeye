[  ] Review the Photographer's Showcase information:
We strongly encourage applicants to fully explore the potential of this site and read the documentation that is provided online. The more you, as a potential exhibiting photographer, understand the site and the information available, the more you will get out of this experience.

[  ] Edit Your Work:
Determine the work you would like to submit for jurying, keeping in mind the following points. The Photographer's Showcase is looking for quality, personal fine-art photography that utilizes photographic methods and techniques, either exclusively or in conjunction with other artistic processes, and not commercial or stock photography. The successful portfolios present a cohesive body of work that demonstrates a freshness of vision.

[  ] Make Scans of Your Work:
Submit work as digital images according to the specifications outlined in the Scans Specifications section of this site.

[  ] Prepare an Artist Résumé:
Include your education, exhibitions, awards, and any additional information that indicates your commitment to photography.

[  ] Prepare an Artist Statement:
Include a short statement (three paragraphs or less) about your body of work and/or what you explore as an artist. If these images are a visual narrative, please give the jurors some background information.

[  ] Prepare a Process Statement:
Describe the technical process(es) you use in your work. Include any camera or darkroom techniques that make your work distinctive.

[  ] Compile a Title List:
Enter Titles for your Images. Include Process, Dimensions, and Retail Price.

[  ] Apply Online:
Have the above materials and a credit card number ready.
