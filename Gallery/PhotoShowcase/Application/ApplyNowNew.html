Netscape Navigator or Internet Explorer 4.0 or higher are required to use our Online Application. At this time, applications and submissions are accepted online only using the following application process.
Step 1 - Pay Application Fee
The introductory online application fee is $35 for the first portfolio and $20 for each additional portfolio submitted at the same time. The application fee is based on the number of portfolios submitted, not on how many images are in each portfolio. The maximum number of images per portfolio is 20.

Setup Charge
 If your portfolio(s) is accepted by the jury, the setup charge is $50 for the first portfolio, $25 for each additional.
Exhibition Fee
 If your portfolio(s) is accepted by the jury, the exhibition fee for one year is $10 per image for the first 20 images accepted, $5 for each additional image.
Fee Payment
 Payment of the Showcase Application fee is required before you can continue with the application process. Complete step one by clicking the Apply Now button at the bottom of this page, and submitting your credit card online. If you prefer to pay by phone, complete the payment information by choosing the Alternate Payment's Credit Card on File option. Then call us toll-free 800-227-6941 and ask to pay the Photographer's Showcase Application fee. If you complete the application order form you will have access to the additional steps of the application process. photo-eye will charge your credit card as soon as your web application form is received.


Step 2 - Artist Résumé (recommended)
Include your education, exhibitions, awards, and any additional information that indicates your commitment to photography. If you already have a bio saved in your computer, simply copy and paste the text from that document into the form provided. Don't be concerned about any formatting changes. At this point, photo-eye is only interested in the information.

Step 3 - Artist Statement (recommended)
Include a short statement (three paragraphs or less) about your body of work and/or what you explore as an artist. If these images are a visual narrative, please give the jurors some background information. If you already have a artist statement in a word processing document, simply copy and paste that text into the form provided. Don't be concerned about any formatting changes. At this point, photo-eye is only interested in the information.


Step 4 - Process Statement (recommended)
Describe the technical process(es) you use in your work. Include any camera or darkroom techniques that make your work distinctive. If you already have a process statement in a word processing document, simply copy and paste that text into the form provided. Don't be concerned about any formatting changes. At this point, photo-eye is only interested in the information.

Step 5 - Title List (recommended)
Enter Titles for your Images. Include Process, Dimensions, and Retail Price.

Step 6 - Your Images
Color correct and spot all images before uploading them to the photo-eye server. Your images should be ready to display. After uploading, if your images are accepted, photo-eye will resize them based on Showcase requirements. There may be other charges if your uploaded images need additional work.

Save all images as JPEGS using the .jpg extension. The JPEG compression setting, should be low, around 75%, with the width and/or height set to at least 1,000 pixels (~ 14 inches) at 72 dpi (dots per inch). Each file should average between 100k to 200k in size.

Use the naming convention shown in the example below. Name the images in the order in which you would like the jurors to view your work. This extremely important if your work is a visual narrative.
Name your images as follows:
Image1.jpg (First image to be juried)

Image2.jpg (Second Image to be juried)

Image3.jpg (Third Image to be juried)

And so on ...

(Maximum of 20 images)

The form used for uploading your work to our server is provided in this step.
